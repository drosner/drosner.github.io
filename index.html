Friends Over Residents

Instead of demonstrating business or management experience, Paul leans on political tenure and long-time alliances. Vendors and donors benefit; residents pay the price.

Both the township solicitor and engineer have supported local party campaigns while enjoying favorable treatment and unchecked billing practices.

Bottom Line

  • Insiders first, residents last.
  • Millions to outside vendors with weak oversight.
  • Obstruction of public records requests—at taxpayer expense.

Key Issues

Warehouse Controversy

  • Helped political allies push a massive warehouse; attacked residents who raised concerns.
  • Politically connected landowners leveraged access to advance early approvals.
  • Developers reassured leadership that neighbors were “taken care of.”
  • Application reportedly pulled until after the election—then refiled weeks later.
  • Staged optics at hearings while granting a key vote that aided the developer on appeal.

Abuse of Eminent Domain

Paul led efforts to seize a farmer’s land for a spray field despite community pushback. If it can happen to one resident, it can happen to anyone.

Protecting Donors & Vendors

  • Refused to investigate tens of thousands in questionable invoices from a campaign-donor engineer.
  • Supported legal action against residents who questioned excessive fees.
  • Outside vendor payments obscured in budgets; transparency remains poor.

Hostile to Transparency

Spent significant taxpayer dollars fighting lawful public records requests, reducing accountability while legal bills soared.

Lack of Qualifications

Background in sales; no management or operational experience. Routinely defers to vendors who profit from township contracts.

Public Confidence

Residents deserve leaders who disclose material issues, avoid conflicts, and demonstrate steady judgment in the public interest.
